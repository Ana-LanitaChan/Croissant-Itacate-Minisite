What is an 'Itacate'?

An itacate is one of the most ancient meals from México that is still made in some towns nowadays. Its name came from 'itacatl' (native Mexican language) which means 'meal for travelers' and the meal was prepared for long journeys. This snack is made from butter and white or blue corn flour on a huge pan and filled traditionally with zucchini flowers, vegetables, and meat. It has a crunchy crust outside and a warm and soft texture inside. It could be dressed on the top with cream, cheese, or spicy salsas.
